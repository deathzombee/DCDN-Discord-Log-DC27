skyria 25-Jan-19 03:38 PM
Definitely shoes - get the beginner shoes (usually more reasonably priced) because as a beginner, you'll wreck your shoes a little faster. Go somewhere you can try shoes on - either your gym, if they offer it, or REI or something, and let them help you find shoes that work. There's a lot of things to look for in rock climbing shoes and while you want them to be snug, you don't want them so tight that you don't want to wear them. I'd definitely recommend chalk + chalk bag, especially if you have a tendency to get sweaty hands. Since hands will be connecting with the rock, trying to reduce moisture with chalk will be very helpful on the rock wall. If you end up with ropes, you'll need a harness, belay device, and carabiner, but if the current plan is bouldering, you can hold off on those until needed.
Also, I tend to calculate out how many times a week/month I need to climb to make the membership worth it. At my particular gym, compared to the daily cost or a punch pass, you need to climb at least 2x a week to make the membership worth it. I climb 3, so definitely worth the money.
