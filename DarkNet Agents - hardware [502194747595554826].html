gateherder 18-Oct-18 12:14 PM
@jonah let us know what the quotes come out to be... this is a special stackup for RF performance. If the materials, stackup thickness, finishing thickness, and dialectric selection are not all just-so (in the readme), then the performance will suffer, potentially greatly.
If anyone thinks harware design is "black magic," then they have never been involved with RF hardware design. It's insanely black magic. It makes typical hardware design look like child's play...
also be careful when populating components. PNP is a good idea here. See if the readme specifies Pb, Pb-Free, No-clean, etc, and make a point to use the approach they specify. If they don't, then I would reocmmende a Pb solder with post-wash if possible. (some only do Pb-free these days) The no-clean stuff will likely cause problems over time in the RF analog sections as the residue oxidizes.
Also important is part number selection in the analog sections. If they call out a specific part number for a cap, use it. Don't substitute it with another cap of the same value. It's parasitics (remember the analog electronics quests?) will be different and, again, affect the RF performance.
digital section stuff is who-cares.... but the analog section stuff is extremely sensitive to the tiniest variances.. If each tiny variance causes a 1% deviation from the design's theoretical max performance, then it doesn't take much to loose 15% or more. In my experience sitting next to analog RF designers in a previous job, something like same-value-different-part-number capacitor substitutions could easily cause 3% loss of performance of the whole RF front end.
That said, I probably just scared you off from trying -- didn't mean to do that. By all means try it. I just wanted you make you aware of the delicacy of the RF section...

RF is an extension of high speed design digital principals.. with high speed digital you have to start worrying about reflections (impedance mismatches, use 45deg route corners instead of 90def, etc) and other issues.
