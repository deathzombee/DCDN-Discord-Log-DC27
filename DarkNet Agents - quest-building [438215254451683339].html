michaelansel 17-May-18 10:49 AM
A quest building thought for all y'all @here this morning: all you really need to build a quest is a brainstorming partner and an evening with Google - throw together a list of things you've heard about but never done before ("I read an article about people hiding information in the bitcoin blockchain; I wonder how that works") - find a buddy for brainstorming: a coworker, a fellow DarkNet agent, somebody sitting on the train as you commute to work - steal a brain wherever you can find it - talk with your brainstorming buddy about different ways you could make challenge quests out of that information. Use Google along the way for inspiration. As with all brainstorming, all ideas are valid at this stage - try to have ideas across the spectrum of difficulty, from "wow, that would be soooo easy" to "I...have no idea how a normal mortal being could solve that" - throw out the most difficult idea; it was probably completely unreasonable brainstorming porn - Congratulations, you've got an outline for a (set of) Challenge Quest(s)! - Now, use Google to learn how to complete your own challenge quest
1
But wait, I thought we wanted to build Training Quests? - assume a normal mortal being was faced with your challenge and knew absolutely nothing about the topic (such as yourself). What would you need to teach them to solve the easier side of the challenges? What would you need them to know to be able to self-teach the information needed for the harder challenges? - Did you notice that you just did all this work when you were teaching yourself how to solve the challenge quest? - Congratulations, you've now got one or more Training Quests! Proof: this is literally what Gulo and I did last night and I've now got a pretty good looking challenge quest that scales from "yup, we can do that" to "dear god why" with "all" the necessary training. Our initial idea seed:
network traffic interception quest. Something involving a physical pair of devices cabled together doing heartbeats (and sending traffic) and you are challenged with intercepting the traffic without disrupting the heartbeats
