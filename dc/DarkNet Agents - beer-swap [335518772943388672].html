Jack of Trades 18-Jul-17 09:46 AM
I used homebrewit.com
I got the 5 gallon kit about 12 years ago. Looks like they've improved it
You'll need bottles and a beer kit to get going ~200 startup. Alternatively you can a keg and build yourself a beer fridge
Get water without chlorine in it however is easiest for you and a big pot for the boiling (3gal recommended)
When it's all brewed and you've got it ready for fermenting, you'll need a cool (~75F) dark place (I've used closets and checked temps regularly. I recommend an arduino or similar with a temp sensor and wifi antenna so you can just have it tweet you the temps when it's close to too hot/ cold)
Then you rack it out (there'll be goop in the bottom, dead yeast and stuff) and bottle/ keg it
After adding sugar
Then wait a bit more
Id say 4ish hours to brew, 4 ish to bottle. Setup included
Read the instructions completely, be sure you understand all of it. Should be easy
I did it my first try with no experience.
Good luck
