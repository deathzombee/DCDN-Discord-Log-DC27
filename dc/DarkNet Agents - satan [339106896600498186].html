michaelansel 21-May-19 10:03 AM
Oh man, I wish I had my puzzle building guide finished. The summary is: make puzzles easier or harder by changing the number of potential paths of investigation without confirmation that you are on the right track. Example: in the buried in the blockchain puzzle, there were about 30 or so relevant transactions to investigate and that was considered a hard puzzle (only two people have ever solved it)
I always try to focus on making the actual solution as obvious as possible and then just burying it in some uncertainty. If you make the solution also convoluted, you can run into the problem of the solver coming up with twice as many possibilities and potential solutions, making it near impossible to actually solve
Another helper is checkpoints/confirmation points where the puzzle solver gets a hint that they are on the right track without necessarily reaching the solution. Example: an image stego challenge that not only has data embedded in the color values but also has data hidden in the image metadata. The metadata is easier to find, but only tells you that you are on the right track (you found the right image)
In my opinion, the best troll is when you make a clever wordplay at the beginning that doesn’t become clear how helpful it was until the puzzle has been solved. My favorite thing is that lightbulb moment at the end when you realize the answer was right in front of you the whole time, if only you could have seen it!
